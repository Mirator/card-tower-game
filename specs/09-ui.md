# UI

## Game Screen Specification

The active v1 presentation is a compact Black vs Red duel screen built entirely in Phaser. It keeps the battlefield readable, treats the hand as the dominant visual system, and makes the top HUD read like a visible card-flow rail with a reveal-focused stage instead of a generic status bar.

## Layout overview

The screen is divided into 4 zones:

- Top center: compact card-flow strip with player draw pile, player discard pile, turn label, enemy castle/wall summary, enemy discard pile, hidden enemy hand, turn chips, and a larger reveal stage that only appears during staged enemy card moments
- Left side: `Black` player panel
- Right side: `Red` player panel
- Bottom: centered 6-card hand lane bounded by a compact selected-card rail on the left and a compact action rail on the right

The background remains a painted Phaser battlefield and the center playfield stays mostly clear during normal turns.

## 1. Top center area

The top strip now acts as the shared card-flow rail:

- Face-down `Black` draw pile with visible count
- Face-up `Black` discard pile with visible top card and count
- Current turn headline
- Compact `Goal + Red C/W` summary
- Face-up `Red` discard pile with visible top card and count
- Persistent hidden `Red` hand rendered as overlapping card backs
- Black/Red turn indicator chips

Behavior:

- Played cards and manual discards travel into the acting side's discard pile
- The player draw pile shows the remaining visible deck count instead of a separate draw preview
- Enemy card reveal appears on a larger framed reveal stage above the battlefield before AI effects resolve
- The AI first highlights one hidden card from the enemy hand, then reveals or discards it
- The reveal-stage dock stays hidden during idle play so the center remains open
- No countdown or turn timer is shown

## 2. Side panels

Both side panels share the same structure and remain visible on desktop and narrow layouts:

- Header color and name: `Black` on the left, `Red` on the right
- Three vertical resource rows:
  - Builders / Bricks
  - Soldiers / Weapons
  - Mages / Crystals
- Castle value
- Wall value

The active side gets a glow treatment so turn ownership is readable without extra text.

## 3. Center battlefield

The battlefield remains the shared combat stage:

- Left tower = Black
- Right tower = Red
- Castle progress meter toward the win condition
- Wall shield visual around each tower
- Low-health danger glow
- Card travel, impact burst, shake, and floating value feedback

Castle height is stylized rather than simulated. Numeric labels and progress fills carry the real state.

## 4. Bottom hand area

The bottom strip is the main interaction surface. On desktop it contains:

- A centered 6-slot visible player hand lane
- A compact selected-card text rail on the left
- A compact Play/Discard action rail on the right
- No persistent battle or event log

On narrow/mobile layouts the same pieces stack more tightly, but the hand remains the largest element in the bottom cockpit.

### Card component structure

Each hand card is a portrait-oriented physical card with:

- A thick rounded outer frame in the card's domain color
- A lighter paper-like inner panel so the card reads as an object, not a flat HUD tile
- Light type-tinted fill for playable cards
- Greyed/desaturated paper fill for unaffordable cards
- Grey border and darker neutral paper tone for unaffordable cards
- Small resource icon in the top-left corner
- Large plain numeric cost in the top-right corner
- Centered bold title in the matching domain color
- A centered illustration motif with a soft shadow so the art feels placed on the card face
- Short 1-2 line effect summary anchored low on the card
- Gold-highlighted selection outline and slight lift when selected and playable

Illustration treatment:

- The active 30-card deck uses explicit illustration keys for simple-but-specific card art
- Building cards lean on wall, masonry, quarry, or tower motifs
- Attack cards use swords, crossed blades, rams, drums, or cracked defenses where appropriate
- Crystal cards use crystals, shields, books, or blasts depending on the card

Color mapping:

- Brick cards: sandstone / ochre
- Weapon cards: crimson red
- Crystal cards: blue
- Unplayable cards: neutral grey
- Green is reserved for positive feedback such as readiness, healing, or gains rather than attack card identity

### Card interaction

Desktop:

- Left click a playable card: plays immediately
- Left click an unplayable card: no action
- Right click a card: discard/cycle it
- Hover: selects and lifts the card
- `Enter`: play selected card
- `Backspace` / `Delete`: discard selected card

Touch:

- Tap: select
- Swipe up: play
- Swipe down: discard
- Play / Discard buttons remain available

States:

- Playable: full color
- Unplayable: grey-framed, darker, and non-committal on click
- Selected: lifted with stronger border and a gold edge treatment
- Unplayable selected: readable for inspection, but stays flat and neutral instead of lifting or glowing

## 5. Selected-card rail

The left rail remains on the bottom edge as a concise fallback inspection surface.

It shows:

- Selected card name
- Current resource ownership versus cost
- Short effect summary

## 6. Action rail

The right rail is an explicit fallback command surface. It stays smaller than the hand so the cards remain the focus.

It shows:

- Selected card name
- Ready or missing-resource status
- Enabled/disabled Play button
- Always-available Discard button during the player action window

## 7. Visual feedback

Required feedback surfaces:

- Floating damage and gain numbers
- Generator change feedback
- Enemy hidden-hand selection before reveal/discard
- Enemy card reveal before AI resolution
- Card travel toward the affected target
- Card travel back into discard piles
- Selected-card preview text before commitment
- No persistent battle/event log in the bottom cockpit

## 8. Phaser implementation mapping

`GameScene` owns:

- `TopCenterContainer`
- `LeftPanelContainer`
- `RightPanelContainer`
- `TowerContainer`
- `HandContainer`
- `OverlayContainer`
- `EndOverlay`

Key UI groups:

- Player panel
- Resource block
- Castle visual
- Player draw pile
- Player discard pile
- Enemy discard pile
- Hidden enemy hand rail
- Hand card view
- Selected-card rail
- Action rail
- Enemy reveal overlay

## 9. UX notes

This layout intentionally keeps the center clear and pushes high-reading-density information to the edges. The hand cards now read like simple physical cards with portrait proportions and paper faces, the top strip shows where cards come from and where they go, the reveal stage turns enemy card moments into short theatrical beats, and the enemy hand is legible as hidden information instead of an invisible AI abstraction.
